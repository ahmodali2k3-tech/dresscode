Party

Stand out,
shine bright

A party is your moment to express personality and have fun with fashion. Whether it's a cocktail soirée or a birthday bash, the key is confidence. Choose pieces that make you feel alive — sequins, bold prints, or a silhouette you love.

✓ Do

  • Wear sequins or metallics
  • Try a bold colour block
  • Statement accessories
  • Heeled boots or strappy heels
  • A chic mini or midi dress

✗ Don't

  • Wear ripped casual jeans
  • Oversized hoodies
  • Flat sneakers (unless party theme)
  • All-black with no texture
  • Ignore the dress code

Office

Power dressing,
refined

Your office attire speaks before you do. Dress with intention — tailored pieces, polished fabrics, and a composed colour palette communicate professionalism and command respect. Smart doesn't have to mean boring.

✓ Do

  • Tailored blazers & trousers
  • Clean, polished footwear
  • Neutral & muted palettes
  • Well-fitted button-down shirts
  • Classic midi skirts

✗ Don't

  • Flip flops or beach sandals
  • Overly casual graphic tees
  • Overly revealing necklines
  • Loud novelty prints
  • Wrinkled or unironed clothes

Wedding

Grace for the
grandest day

Attending a wedding as a guest is an art — you want to honour the occasion and celebrate the couple while looking your absolute best. Remember: the bride is the star. Dress elegantly without stealing the spotlight.

✓ Do

  • Floral midi or maxi dresses
  • Elegant jumpsuits
  • Pastel or jewel tone palette
  • Fascinators or hair accessories
  • Block-heel or wedge shoes

✗ Don't

  • Wear white, cream, or ivory
  • Outshine the wedding party
  • Wear black alone (context matters)
  • Casual jeans or shorts
  • Overly revealing outfits

Casual

Effortless,
everyday style

Casual doesn't mean careless. Elevated everyday dressing is about mixing comfort with intention — quality basics, well-chosen textures, and thoughtful layering that makes you look put-together without trying too hard.

✓ Do

  • Quality denim & linen
  • Crisp white tee with tailored trousers
  • Layer with lightweight knits
  • Clean white sneakers
  • Minimal jewellery

✗ Don't

  • Wear pilled or faded clothing
  • Ill-fitting basics
  • Mix too many bold logos
  • Forget to iron/steam
  • Ignore shoe choice

Black Tie

The art of
true elegance

Black tie is the pinnacle of dress codes — reserved for galas, operas, and formal ceremonies. This is your moment to embody timeless sophistication. Long gowns, classic tuxedo dressing, and impeccable grooming define this occasion.

✓ Do

  • Floor-length gowns
  • Classic tuxedo or dinner suit
  • Fine jewellery & watches
  • Formal heels or patent shoes
  • Evening bags & gloves

✗ Don't

  • Anything shorter than knee-length
  • Casual shoes or boots
  • Overly trendy or novelty pieces
  • Jeans in any form
  • Costume jewellery

Universal Rules

The golden rules
of dressing well

01
Fit is everything

The most expensive outfit looks cheap if it doesn't fit. Invest in a tailor — even basic pieces look elevated when they're fitted to your body.

02
Dress the occasion

Always consider the venue, the host, and the guests. Being overdressed shows respect; being underdressed shows you didn't care enough to try.

03
Quality over quantity

Three exceptional pieces outperform thirty mediocre ones. Build a capsule wardrobe of well-made basics and rotate intentionally.

04
Shoes make the outfit

Even the simplest look transforms with the right shoe. Pay attention — your choice of footwear signals your level of care more than any other piece.

05
Colour harmony

Limit your outfit to three colours maximum. A dominant, secondary, and accent. This formula creates effortless coordination without overthinking.

06
Confidence is the final accessory

Wear every outfit with intent. Walk tall. The most stylish people aren't wearing the most expensive clothes — they're wearing whatever they choose with absolute conviction.
